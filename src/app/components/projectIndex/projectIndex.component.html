Index Project



Have I said I like to learn new languages? In fact, I decided when I was 25 that every five years I would start learning a new one. Now I am learning Korean and In a couple of years I think maybe Russian or German will be next.
I had a problem though. I don't have much time to study languages, and it is specially difficult for me to remember and practice the vocabulary I just learnt. There are some websites and apps that helps you with that, but I couldn't find anything that perfectly suits my needs and I was also learning how to develop Android apps, so 1+1.

Overview


The main idea is: you create some languages, add some words and study or do tests. In figure 1 you can see I have two languages created, one in gold (favourite always displayed at top).

It doesn't have to be languages though, can be books you are studying or whatever could better fit your interests. When I started developing this app, I wanted it to be like some kind of electronic notebook that allows you to store the words but also study them, efficiently search them and make tests. Therefore this is dummy data, I will not have languages here but notebooks, maybe one per book.


Data storage




Figure 2 shows us how the words are displayed once saved in the database. We can of course filter the data to search for any specific word. Here you can also mark them to study or not and see the results in your tests, so you know which words you have to pay atention to. Although there is not screen of this, you can touch a word to enter the 'word detail section' in which all info is displayed and can be altered.

The screen to add new words can be seen in Figure 3. The difficulty and categories are useful for filters and tests. You can add zero, one or more categories to the word and choose between easy, medium or difficult.

The last screen in Figure 4 is related to create new Categories. The reason for categories to exist is obvious. I would like to classify the words to choose what do I want to study, or to make searchs easier when trying to find a word. Categories can be language/notebook specific or global, it depends on what the user choose in options.

Study section




When enter into the study section, a screen like the one in Figure 5 appears. I created this view because I wanted to be able to see only one of the two columns when studying. If i want to check a word, just clicking in the buttons above will show/hide the specified column. No more hands covering the answers.

If we go to the Create test screen (figure 6), there are two options. First one is simple, just do a simple test by clicking 'quickstart' (the parameters for quickstart can be configured in the options of course). Second one is choosing what do we want to study, choosing the options, adding categories and clicking in 'start test'.
In both cases, an algorithm decides which words will appear in the test, and it depends on how much time did pass before last time you studied that word, how difficult it is, what is your success ratio and a bit of random.

Lastly after doing the test we can see the results in Figure 7. Did I said I am good at remembering words?

What's next


First of all, the app need to be fixed because there are some strange bugs that happens from time to time. Also the interface must be polished, and some features to make it more user friendly should be added.

After that, I am planning to completely change the way it's done. Right now I am using an Android database and everything is stored in the mobile.
The idea is to separate the frontend from the backend by storing the data in a database in another computer, and access it by web services. Therefore it will be easier to make versions for web, desktop or other mobile devices, and also practicing a the same time new technologies.
