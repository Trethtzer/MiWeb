1. THEA The Awakening.


This is a kind of game that you cannot find on your own, unless you are really lucky. It is not very well known and in fact I am lucky to know it because a friend of mine did play in the past and recommended it to me. It is a survival strategy game, turn based, in which combat is resolved by playing a card game, being your characters a card with different statistics. To complete the game you have to finish the main story, which will make you face different types of encounters and problems.

Why survival then? Well, if you make mistakes, your characters will suffer the consecuences. Like darkest dungeon, your characters can get ill, cursed, injured and even die. And you never know what you will have to face, maybe you enter a dungeon to get some loot, and would find either normal skeletons or a group of specters and vampires (and in that case you better be prepared or may end up starting a new game).

There is one nice thing though about starting a new game. Your colony worships a god, but there are different gods, and you can choose one at the begining of the game. Different gods give different bonuses, and when you progress in the game with your god, you will unlock new powers for future games. By the way, the game is not long, so it is not that rare to start a new game after finishing it.

You may think that this is boring, (in fact even though I don´t like to replay games, I already did it twice!) but in this game there are different reasons to do so:
  • First of all, it doesn´t have to be straight after, you may play other games and come back months or years later.
  • The difficulty the game is COMPLETELY customizable, and depending on how difficult you set the options (it is calculated with a percentage) your god will gain more or less experience.
  • There are tons of events, it is imposible to see all of them in a game (in fact in my games I think I only saw 10%, there is a statistic for this). So if you start a new game with a different god, the god questline will be different and also the events that you will face will not be the same.


It is a story - roleplaying game. Like in the photo, all the events will be similar, they will show you a text with some options and you will have to choose. In fact, there is even an audio for all the events, so you can just relax, close your eyes and imagine the situation that is coming through your ears. It seems like a stupid thing but beleive me, it is really appreciated, and helps you put in the situation and make better decisions.

Also the options depends on your party, some times you will see grey-out options, that you cannot choose because you don´t have a special character in your party to do that. (For example you may need a dwarf to negotiate with other dwarfs, or a mage/witch to do some magic and make a troll sleep instead of fighting it). This kind of things make this game outstand and shine.


And also, for people who likes hardcore games, there are two options which combined makes the game almost impossible to beat, unless you are a strategic expert and have your god at max level. This options are ironman (you cannot reload the game from an earlier point, if a character dies, it dies forever) and bloodbath, which changes the way the injures work. It make it more realistic, basically the math behind the injuries change to have in consideration really big hits. For example, let's suppose some orcs attack and reduce the life of one of your characters to 0, and the same does a super big giant, who hits the character with a giant stone. In the normal mode, in both cases the character has 0 life and will have around 70% chance to die, unless you have a medic in your party (or someone with medic points), which will reduce this death possibility. But in bloodbath mode probably the character will have -5 life and in the case of the giant, -70 life (it was smashed by a giant stone dude). To not die from this hit, after the first turn you will have like 90% chance to die, but if you survive, then your life will be -65 or so, so again you have 88% to die. In the end obviously the char dies, like in real life if a stone that big makes you human omelette.

In conclusion, I think is a game worth a try if you like strategy, roleplaying or hardcore games. And also is quite cheap! If you are already attracted by what I wrote, I am pretty sure you will not regret throwing 5 - 10 euros to this game. Maybe you don´t worship this game after all, but at least you will have a good time for a really low price. Hope you like it!
